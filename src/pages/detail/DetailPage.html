Trash

  • What Time?

    Household garbage must be put inside the city-provided container which should be placed at the curb between 6 p.m. the day before and 7 a.m. on the scheduled collection day.

  • Where to Put Your Bin

    The container must be accessible to the automated collection truck and not blocked by obstacles, including parked cars. Drivers will not move improperly placed containers.

Yard Waste

  • About Yard Waste

    Yard Trimmings, grass clippings, small branches and leaves must not be placed in the automated garbage cans. They must be in city-approved compostable bags not weighing more than 50 pounds, and placed at the curb 3 feet away from the automated container for separate yard trimmings collection.

  • Branches

    Small branches may be put in bundles as long as each bundle is less than 4 feet in length and 18 inches in diameter and weighing less than 50 pounds.

  • Don't Mix!

    Yard Trimmings mixed with paper, plastic, or any other type of waste will not be collected.

  • Contractors

    Work performed by a contractor must be removed by the contractor.

Recycling

  • What Can I Recycle?

    Newspapers, magazines, and other paper items. Most plastics (#1 - 5 and 7), aluminum and tin cans.
    Keep your recyclables clean for easy processing!

  • What About Glass?

    Sorry, glass is currently not allowed in the bins. You can use Drop Off Locations.

    Another option is iRecycle Glass a paid service for glass pickup!

Heavy Trash/Junk Waste

  • What's Junk Waste?

    “Junk waste” is used to describe items such as furniture, appliances and other bulky materials. These items should be placed adjacent to the front curb in a location eas- ily accessible to the collection vehicle between the hours of 6:00 p.m. the Friday before, and 7:00 a.m. on the scheduled col- lection day.

  • How Much Can I Put Out?

    No more than eight cubic yards per residence will be collected on the scheduled tree waste/junk waste collection day. Of this amount, only a maximum of four cubic yards of building shingles, brick, plaster or con- crete) generated by the resident in connection with the mainte- nance of the residential property may be collected by department personnel.

Tree Waste

What's Tree Waste?

“Tree waste”, which consists of tree limbs, branches or stumps. Lumber, furniture and treated wood will not be accepted.
